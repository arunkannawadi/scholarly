12class PublicationSource(str, Enum): 

13 ''' 

14 Defines the source of the publication. In general, a publication  

15 on Google Scholar has two forms: 

16 * Appearing as a PUBLICATION SNIPPET and 

17 * Appearing as a paper in an AUTHOR PAGE 

18  

19 ------------ 

20  

21 "PUBLICATION SEARCH SNIPPET".  

22 This form captures the publication when it appears as a "snippet" in  

23 the context of the resuls of a publication search. For example: 

24  

25 Publication search: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?hl=en&q=adaptive+fraud+detection&btnG=&as_sdt=0%2C33 

26  

27 The entries appear under the <div class = "gs_r gs_or gs_scl"> tags 

28 Each entry has a data-cid attribute (e.g., data-cid="pthm1bWT96oJ") 

29  

30 The same type of results will also appear when someome searches  

31 using the "cited by", "related articles", and "all XX versions" links 

32 that appear under the publication snippet. 

33  

34 "Cited By" link: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?cites=12319477714873931942&as_sdt=5,33&sciodt=0,33&hl=en 

35  

36 "Related Articles" link: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=related:pthm1bWT96oJ:scholar.google.com/&scioq=adaptive+fraud+detection&hl=en&as_sdt=0,33 

37  

38 "All versions" link: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?cluster=12319477714873931942&hl=en&as_sdt=0,33 

39  

40 The snippet version of these publications contain the information that appears in the results. 

41 Often, the snippet version will miss authors, will have an abbreviated name for the venue, and so on. 

42  

43 We can fill these snippets by clicking on the "Cite" button" and get back the MLA/APA/Chicago/...  

44 citations forms, PLUS links for BibTeX, EndNote, RefMan, and RefWorks. 

45  

46 ------------ 

47 "AUTHOR PUBLICATION ENTRY" 

48  

49 We also have publications that appear in the "author pages" of Google Scholar.  

50 These publications are often a set of publications "merged" together.  

51  

52 The snippet version of these publications conains the title of the publication, 

53 a subset of the authors, the (sometimes truncated) venue, and the year of the publication 

54 and the number of papers that cite the publication. 

55  

56 The snippet entries appear under the <tr class="gsc_a_tr"> entries in the main page of the author. 

57  

58 To fill in the publication, we open the "detailed view" of the paper 

59  

60 Detailed view page: https://scholar.google.com/citations?view_op=view_citation&hl=en&citation_for_view=-Km63D4AAAAJ:d1gkVwhDpl0C 

61 ''' 

62 PUBLICATION_SEARCH_SNIPPET = "PUBLICATION_SEARCH_SNIPPET" 

63 AUTHOR_PUBLICATION_ENTRY = "AUTHOR_PUBLICATION_ENTRY"

134 (source: AUTHOR_PUBLICATION_ENTRY) 

135 :param citedby_url: This corresponds to a "single" publication on Google Scholar. Used in the web search

138 If the publication comes from a "merged" list of papers from an authors page,

141 https://scholar.google.com/scholar?cluster=16766804411681372720&hl=en 

142 :param cites_per_year: a dictionay containing the number of citations per year for this Publication 

143 (source: AUTHOR_PUBLICATION_ENTRY) 

144 :param eprint_url: digital version of the Publication. Usually it is a pdf. 

145 :param pub_url: url of the website providing the publication

149 of multiple publications, and therefore may have multiple "citedby_id" 

150 values. 

151 (source: AUTHOR_PUBLICATION_ENTRY) 

152 :param url_related_articles: the url containing link for related articles of a publication (needs fill() for AUTHOR_PUBLICATION_ENTRIES) 

153 :param url_add_sclib: (source: PUBLICATION_SEARCH_SNIPPET) 

154 :param url_scholarbib: the url containing links for  

155 the BibTeX entry, EndNote, RefMan and RefWorks (source: PUBLICATION_SEARCH_SNIPPET) 

156 :param filled: whether the publication is fully filled or not 

157 :param source: The source of the publication entry 

158 :param container_type: Used from the source code to identify if this container object
